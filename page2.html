    *Image: problem 15 from the quiz on Chapter 1-3

    Preparing For Quiz: What I do to review for the quiz is study the terms and formulas. Normally, I write down terms and formulas in a notebook that is dedicated to math but because the course is online and I decided I would not be wasting paper just to write down terms and formulas, I use Paint 3D (app on laptop) to write them down instead. Paint 3D is like a whiteboard for me. Furthermore, I saved copies of the homework to practice doing the problems that I struggle with. Sometimes, I watch YouTube videos to help me understand how to calculate a problem or define the terms.

    Materials/Tools: A material I find useful is the formula sheet. There are many formulas to remember. So, having the formula sheet is convenient for referencing which formula to use. A useful tool would be the Texas Instrument TI-Nspire CX II calculator used to calculate numbers and graphing. It is similar to the TI-84 but the settings to calculate things are different. I had to figure out how to calculate with the different settings on my own.

    Reflection: After seeing my quiz grade, I think about what I did wrong on the problem and what I can do better on the next quiz. There are simple mistakes I make just after reading the problem. Basically, I misinterpret what the problem is asking for. I have to be extra cautious when reading the problem.
